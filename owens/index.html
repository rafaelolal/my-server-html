Jesse Owens

James Cleveland "Jesse" Owens was an african american track and field athlete. Born in Oakville, Alabama on on September 12, 1913, Owens is thought to be one of the greatest and most famous athletes in history. Owens accomplished many astonishing feats during his life, first coming to national attention when he matched 2 world records as student of East Technical High School. Owens would continue his athletic success at the Ohio State University, but suffered discrimination when he was seperated off campus with black athletes and was denied scholarships despite his achievements. In 1935 while attending a Big Ten meeting an University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, he achieved three world records in the long jump (8.13 m), 220 yard sprint (20.3 seconds) and the 220 yard low hurdle (22.6 seconds) as well as matching the record for the 100-yard dash (9.4 seconds). A year later he would gain international fame while competing in the 1936 Olymic games in Nazi Germany, fighting not just for the medals, but against Nazi Germany's ideology of Aryan supremacy. Owens went on to become the most successful athlete at the games, winning 4 gold medals and toppling Hitler's dream of German victory.

Alas, even with the international fame Owens' garnered during from his olympic achievement, the remainder of his life was not as illustrious, as his color denied him many privileges that Olympic athletes recieved, and he was forced to work menial jobs in order to get by. Owens died lung cancer at the age of 66 on March 31, 1980. After his death, Owens is still remembered for his record breaking athletic achievement, and his fearless opposition of Nazi opression.
