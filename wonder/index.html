STEVIE WONDER



"We all have ability. The difference is how we use it."


Stevie Wonder is a world-renowned singer, songwriter, musician, record producer, and activist. Born Stevland Judkins on May 13, 1950, he showed a remarkable talent for music at a young age, and was regarded as a child prodigy. In the early 1960s, a young Stevie was discovered by producer Berry Gordy Jr., who signed him to Motown Records at age 11. Wonder later became a sensation with his eclectic blend of soul, R&B, and pop music. Some of his classic hits include "Superstition," "Signed, Sealed, Delivered I'm Yours," and "Isn't She Lovely."


Over the years, Stevie has won numerous awards and accolades for his art, including 25 Grammy Awards, making him one of the most successful and influential musicians in history. He has also been a prominent social activist in and out of music, consistently addressing issues such as racism, poverty, and inequality. A notable act is his successful campaign to make Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s birthday a national holiday, which included public rallies and the creation of his 1980 record "Happy Birthday."


Stevie is also known for his philanthropic work and has been involved in many charitable causes, including the fight against HIV/AIDS and the support of children's education. For his work, he was honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the United Nations' Messenger of Peace award.


Today, Stevie Wonder continues to perform and record music. His influence can be heard in the work of countless artists in a wide range of genres. His contributions to music and society have made him a true icon, and his legacy will continue to inspire generations to come.
