Jackie Robinson

The Life and Legacy of Jackie Robinson

“I'm not concerned with your liking or disliking me… all I ask is that you respect me as a human being.”

  • Jackie Robinson (1919-1972) was an American professional baseball player who broke the color barrier in Major League Baseball when he signed with the Brooklyn Dodgers in 1947
  • Was a talented athlete and also excelled in football, basketball, and track and field
  • Faced constant racial discrimination and hostility from some fans and fellow players, he still became a six-time All-Star and was Rookie of the Year in 1947
  • He was also a civil rights activist and a symbol of hope and progress for generations to come
  • He also worked closely with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. at rallies, fundraising events, and marches--using his prestige to advocate for equality
