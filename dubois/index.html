W. E. B. Du Bois

  • One of the founders of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP)

  • A leader of the Niagara Movement, a group of African-American activists who wanted equal rights for blacks

  • Strongly protested against lynching, Jim Crow laws, and discrimination in education and employment

  • Wrote lots of works, including "Black Reconstruction in America," a book that challeneged the prominent view that African Americans were to blame for the failures during the Reconstruction Era

  • Believed that capitalism was a primary cause of racism

“I believe in pride of race and lineage and self; in pride of self so deep as to scorn injustice to other selves.”

- W. E. B. Du Bois
