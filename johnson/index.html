MARSHA P. JOHNSON

A pioneer in the U.S. LGBTQ+ rights movement

August 24, 1945 (Elizabeth, NJ) - July 6, 1992 (New York, NY)

  • A powerful black transgender woman
  • Self-identified as gay, transvestite, and a drag queen at the time
  • Most known for activism during the Stonewall Riots
  • Advocated for LGBTQ+ youth
  • Fought for those with HIV/AIDS


"Pay it no mind," in response to questions of her gender.

EARLY LIFE

  • Born on August 24, 1945, in Elizabeth, NJ
  • Assigned male at birth
  • Fifth of seven children in a middle-class family
  • Her father was Malcolm Michaels Sr., and her mother was Alberta Claiborne
  • Her family was religious and went to Mount Teman African Methodist Episcopal Church
  • She stayed Christian until her death
  • She wore women's clothes at the age of five but got bullied. She was even sexually assaulted by a thirteen-year-old boy
  • She graduated Thomas A. Edison High School and moved to New York City with nothing except $15 and a single bag of clothing

POST-HIGH SCHOOL LIFE

  • Expressed herself once again by wearing women's clothing; wore a flower crown often
  • Used she/her pronouns and changed her name to Marsha P. Johnson, with the middle initial "P" for "Pay It No Mind"
  • She did not identify as transgender at the time (gay, transvestite, and a drag queen), yet many refer to her as transgender since the term was only popularized after Marsha's life
  • Turned to sex work due to struggles with LGBTQ+ rights and employment
  • Got abused by sex work clients; arrested often
  • No stable home; stayed with friends or at hotels, restaurants, and theaters
  • Found jobs as a waitress and drag show performer
  • Became friends with 11-year-old transgender Puerto Rican girl named Sylvia Rivera, who was like her child
  • She stayed close with family, but the ties were also uneasy

ACTIVISM, IMPACT, AND LATE LIFE

  • On June 28, 1969, The Stonewall Inn (a gay bar) was raided by police in the morning, arresting many people
  • She and Sylvia went at 2am and saw the Stonewall Riots occurring
  • Unsure of her exact role, but she stayed in the front lines
  • She led many protests along with Sylvia
  • The Stonewall Riots led to progression in LGBTQ+ rights, such as the first Gay Pride Parade in 1970
  • She and Sylvia founded Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries (STAR) to help kicked out transgender youth
  • They created the STAR House to give a home to these youths
  • Throughout the 1970's, she performed with "Hot Peaches," a drag group, went to many interviews, and even rode the lead car in the 1980 Gay Pride Parade
  • She experienced many breakdowns and went to many psychiatric hospitals; also continued sex work for a job
  • Was diagnosed with HIV in 1990 and advocated for others with the disease
  • She died on July 6, 1992. Her body was discovered in the Hudson River; it was deemed a suicide, but many suspected it was not
  • The police did not investigate further until reopening the case in 2012
  • She was loved: hundreds went to her funeral, crowding not only the church but the streets
  • New York City commissioned a monument for Marsha and Sylvia in 2019, and in 2020, a waterfront park in Brooklyn was named after Marsha
  • There are documentaries such as Pay It No Mind: Marsha P. Johnson about her life
