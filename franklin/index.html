Aretha Franklin

March 25th, 1942 - August 16th, 2018


Biography The Queen of Soul Music
  • Aretha began her singing career when she was referred to as a child prodigy during her father's church services.
  • At the young age of 14, she was then offered to record a few songs and released her first ep titled Songs of Faith.
  • Later towards her adulthood, she signed up with a recording company, Atlantic Records, and was allowed to have creative control over her music. She began to focus on releasing gospel music and blues songs where she began to increase in popularity.
  • From her popularity, she became known as “Lady Soul” and was nationally recognized as having one of the most influential songs during the Civil Rights Movement titled, “Respect”. She continued to release some of the biggest hits and was loved by everyone.
    		•
  • Recognized as The Queen of Soul for her empowerment and help during the civil rights movement
  • Sang “Precious Lord” during Martin Luther King Jr.’s Funeral.
  • Sang the national anthem at the Democratic Convention.
  • In 1987, she had become the first African American woman to be inducted into the “Rock and Roll Hall of Fame”.
  • Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2005).
